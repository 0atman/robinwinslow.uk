--- title: Galaxy Nexus still hasn't received Jelly Bean OTA update in the UK layout: post type: regular ---

A friend of mine just ordered a new Google Nexus from Amazon (for about £300 I think). Even though it's now almost exactly 9 months since the phone was released in the UK, it's still the latest official Google phone and a very attractive package for the price.

One of the real advantages of a Google phone is of course that you're the first to get official Android updates, e.g. the new Jelly Bean release. But even though the update was released over the air to Nexus 7 at the beginning of July, the Galaxy Nexus didn't update to it.

We spent ages trying to work out if there was something wrong with the phone. We discovered that there are multiple variations of the phone, and that his variant is the GT-i9250, which is the international HSPA+ model. According to the BBC, this model should have been the first type of Galaxy Nexus to receive the new update.

Eventually we thought to look at the official support channels, and found that in fact Google has an absolutely fantastic Call Back feature for support of Nexus devices. After being on hold for less than a minute my friend spent about 15 minutes trying a couple of solutions with the support of the very friendly support people, before discovering that in fact Jelly Bean has not been pushed to any Galaxy Nexus devices in the UK yet, but that it should be pushed soon.

I'm a bit disappointed with how far the UK seems to be behind the US in getting this release, but very impressed with the level of support Google offer for their device.
